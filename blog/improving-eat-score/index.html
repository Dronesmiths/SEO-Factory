How to Improve Your E-A-T Score

Improving your E-A-T score is a long-term investment in your website's authority and user trust. Here are the key steps to take.

Step 1: Audit Your Authors

Verify that every piece of content on your site has a clearly identified author with a bio that highlights their relevant experience and expertise.

Step 2: Citations and References

Back up your claims with citations from high-authority sources. This demonstrates that your content is thoroughly researched and authoritative.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a numerical E-A-T score?

No, Google does not assign a single numerical E-A-T score to websites. It's a qualitative assessment.

How long does it take to improve E-A-T?

It can take several months to see the effects of E-A-T improvements as search engines re-evaluate your site's reputation.

Do external links help E-A-T?

Linking out to authoritative sources can help demonstrate transparency, but it's the quality of your own content that matters most.

Should I remove low-quality content?

Pruning thin or outdated content can significantly improve the overall E-A-T signals of your domain.

What is the role of citations?

Citations provide evidence that your information is accurate and supported by established facts or expertise.
